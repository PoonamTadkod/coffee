Mogra Information: The Mogra plant (Jasminum sambac) is a species of jasmine that is native to South Asia and Southeast Asia. It is a small shrub or vine with fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom in the summer and fall. Mogra plants are prized for their fragrant flowers, which are used in perfumes, religious ceremonies, and to make garlands and other decorative items. The Mogra plant is relatively easy to grow and can be propagated through stem cuttings.It can be grown in a container or in the ground, and it may need to be trained or supported as it grows. The plant should be watered regularly, but not too much, as over-watering can lead to root rot. The Mogra plant is a popular ornamental plant and is often grown in gardens and homes for its fragrance and beauty.

lotus flower may not seem like much to think about at first glance, but first impressions can be misleading. While the plant's symmetry and colors are surely striking, beneath the delicate exterior of a lotus lies a deeper meaning nearly as old as time. In many Eastern cultures the lotus holds great symbolic weight and is considered one of the most sacred plants in the world. So, what is it about this specific bloom that makes it oh so special? First and foremost, the lotus has a life cycle unlike any other plant. With its roots latched in mud, it submerges every night into river water and miraculously re-blooms the next morning, sparklingly clean. As a result, this has led the lotus to have associations as the flower with rebirth and spiritual enlightenment. With its daily process of life, death, and reemergence, it's no wonder that the lotus holds such symbolism.
